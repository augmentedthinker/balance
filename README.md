# Balance

Balance is a personal life-navigation app organized around nine domains, with **Spirit** at the center, designed to help Christopher make more aligned real-world decisions about where time, energy, and attention should go.

## Purpose

Balance is being developed as a personal life-architecture tool rather than a generic self-improvement dashboard.

The goal is to create a system that helps Christopher:
- divide life into meaningful domains
- assess those domains honestly
- clarify what each domain is really asking
- use the structure to support real decision-making
- gradually evolve from reflection into intervention

The emerging core question is:

> **What is the most aligned use of my next hour?**

## Current Core Domains

- Mind
- Body
- Impact
- Connection
- Spirit
- Home
- Novelty
- Play
- Wealth

## Current Domain Clarification Layer

The current menu now includes a plain-language interpretive prompt for each domain:

- **Mind** → Am I engaging my intellect meaningfully?
- **Body** → Am I physically alive and capable?
- **Impact** → Am I producing something that matters beyond me?
- **Connection** → Am I meaningfully engaged with people?
- **Spirit** → Am I aligned with who I am and why I’m doing this?
- **Home** → Does my environment support me?
- **Novelty** → Am I experiencing anything new?
- **Play** → Am I enjoying being alive?
- **Wealth** → Am I handling reality and resources responsibly?

These are important because they move the app closer to a usable decision surface rather than a purely aesthetic or philosophical map.

## Current Conceptual Model

### Center
- **Spirit** is the interpretive center of the system.

### Key evaluative logic
- the earlier key internal rating concept remains **contentment**, not raw productivity or surface performance
- the newer conceptual pressure is toward helping the system influence **behavior**, not merely display reflection

### Strategic shift
The app is increasingly being understood as a system that should help **interrupt drift**.

That means the direction is shifting:
- from **mirror → compass**
- from **static philosophy → dynamic action**
- from **tracking → intervention**

### Role of AI / agent layer
The intended agent role is not to invent Christopher’s values, but to help execute and interpret them.

Working principle:

> **AI should function as a loyal lieutenant, not a philosopher king.**

## Philosophical Lens Layer

The app is designed so each domain can be interpreted through different philosophical or spiritual lenses.

Current active lenses:
- Stoicism
- Zen Buddhism
- Existentialism
- Christianity

These lenses are meant to deepen interpretation, not replace Christopher’s own definitions.

## Current State of the Repo

- domain clarification questions listed beneath the menu
- individual pages for each life domain
- baseline domain descriptions based on Christopher-authored domain definitions

## Design Direction So Far

The current visual and product direction emphasizes:
- contemplative but practical presentation
- mobile usability alongside Chromebook/desktop review
- a centered 3×3 life-domain architecture
- philosophical depth without drifting into vagueness
- personal usefulness before premature generalization

## Near-Term Direction

Likely next steps include:
- simplifying the app further as needed
- translating domain structure into action-shaping behavior
- deciding how ratings, recency, and momentum should eventually work
- defining what interventions or prompts would actually be useful
- determining what a first real check-in loop should look like
- exploring how OpenClaw or cron-based agent support could participate later
